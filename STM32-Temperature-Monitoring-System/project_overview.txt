PROJECT TITLE: STM32 Temperature Monitoring System
AUTHOR: Rupa Mane

ENGINEERING FLOW & ARCHITECTURE:

1. Peripheral Clock Configuration: 
   - Initialized the RCC (Reset and Clock Control) to enable clocks for GPIOA, ADC1, and USART2.
2. ADC Configuration: 
   - Set the sampling time and resolution to 12-bit to ensure accurate readings from the LM35 sensor.
3. UART Parameterization: 
   - Configured the word length (8 bits), stop bits (1), and parity (None) to match the PC terminal settings.
4. Firmware Logic: 
   - Developed a super-loop architecture to continuously monitor environmental changes and provide immediate LED feedback.

TECHNICAL CHALLENGES & SOLUTIONS:

- The Scaling Challenge: 
  Converting a raw binary ADC value into a human-readable temperature required precise floating-point math to avoid rounding errors during the 12-bit conversion.
- Signal Integrity: 
  Addressed potential fluctuations in ADC readings by introducing a sampling delay, allowing the sensor signal to stabilize between read cycles.
- Pin Multiplexing: 
  Carefully configured the Pinout in STM32CubeIDE to ensure that the USART2 TX function on PA2 did not conflict with other required hardware peripherals.

KEY LEARNINGS:
- Deep understanding of HAL (Hardware Abstraction Layer) drivers for STM32.
- Expertise in interpreting hardware datasheets for voltage-to-temperature scaling.
- Proficiency in hardware debugging using serial terminal feedback (Tera Term/UART).
